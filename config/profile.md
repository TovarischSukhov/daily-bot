# Reader profile

## Who I am
Founder / entrepreneur. I write on LinkedIn for an audience of founders,
e-commerce operators, e-commerce leadrs, e-commerce founders and for fintech\b2c apps leaders (c-lvl and product). I run two businesses:

- **ASAO DS** (asaods.com) — boutique data & AI consulting. Data advisory
  (audits, roadmaps, fractional data leadership) + building ML/AI systems
  (recommenders, data warehouses, BI). Clients are scale-ups in
  fintech, e-commerce, and consumer apps. Led by ex-data execs who shipped data
  products at scale.
- **ob.session** (ob-session.com) — a real-time e-commerce personalization
  engine. Recommends products from live on-site behavior (what shoppers pause,
  hover, scroll), with no login, history, or cookies — privacy by design. We're
  now expanding into the e-commerce market, so e-com reach matters to me.

## What I want from the digest
1. Material I can write LinkedIn posts about for the e-commerce world. ob.session is our main priority right now
2. Staying aware of tech (AI + mainstream), and business/macro/politics *only*
   where it actually moves business.

## Weighting
The core is the **life of the e-commerce community and business** — what
merchants, DTC brands, marketplaces, and retail operators are actually doing,
worrying about, and arguing over. AI and tech are tools I use, not the main
thing I want to read or write about; weight them down unless there's a real
e-com angle.

- **HIGH** (importance and content_potential): how e-commerce and retail
  businesses operate and grow — merchant and DTC brand stories, marketplace and
  channel shifts (Amazon, Shopify, TikTok Shop, etc.), conversion / retention /
  pricing / fulfillment / CAC, customer behavior, the founder and operator life
  in e-com, retail-industry moves and consumer-spending trends. Personalization
  and applied data/AI count here *only when framed for an e-commerce business*.
- **MEDIUM**: general AI/LLM and mainstream tech news (model releases, big-tech
  moves, data tooling) — relevant to my work but surface it only when there's a
  clear angle for an e-com audience; macro/business/politics when it clearly hits
  e-commerce, retail, or startups (tariffs, regulation, consumer spending).
- **LOW**: tech/AI news with no e-com angle, consumer gadget reviews,
  celebrity/funding gossip, crypto price moves, "AI will change everything"
  takes, and politics that doesn't touch business.
